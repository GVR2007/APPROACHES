Conventional Water
Treatment Evaluation

A formal lecture-style breakdown of a seven-stage purification system (Non-RO), optimized for sustainable pond water treatment with high mineral retention and zero industrial wastage.

STRATEGIC OVERVIEW

System Conceptual Mind Map

INFRASTRUCTURE COMPONENT 01

Screening

Capex: ₹500 – ₹2,000
Conceptual Schematic Village Implementation

Methodology & Operation

Coarse physical barrier (mesh or bars) at the inlet that removes large debris like leaves, twigs, plastics, and floating solids before water enters the sedimentation tank. It is the first defensive line preventing clogging of downstream units.

Pros

  • Completely passive
  • Prevents damage
  • Low maintenance

Cons

  • Size restricted
  • Requires manual cleaning

Evaluator's Rationale

Essential first step for raw pond water with high debris/turbidity. It protects the 1000 L sedimentation tank and keeps the whole system efficient at minimal cost.

INFRASTRUCTURE COMPONENT 02

Sedimentation Tank (1000L)

Capex: ₹5,000 – ₹15,000
Conceptual Schematic Village Implementation

Methodology & Operation

Gravity-based settling where water is held still, allowing heavier suspended particles, silt, and some organics to settle at the bottom as sludge. Performance data indicates bulk removal of suspended load.

Pros

  • Simple concrete/HDPE
  • High silt removal
  • Passive settling

Cons

  • High retention time
  • Sludge buildup

Perfect low-cost way to handle high initial turbidity in pond water. Reduces burden on biological and membrane stages, aligning with the "low energy, no wastage" goal.

INFRASTRUCTURE COMPONENT 03

Diffused Aeration Unit

Capex: ₹8,000 – ₹25,000
Conceptual Schematic Village Implementation

Methodology & Operation

Air is bubbled through diffusers at the bottom, increasing dissolved oxygen (DO). This oxidises iron (makes it filterable), strips volatile gases/odours, and reduces ammonia through volatilisation or enhanced biological activity.

Pros

  • Natural oxidation
  • Enhanced DO
  • Odor control

Cons

  • Electric load
  • Equipment noise

Evaluator's Rationale

Critical for pond water issues (low DO, odour, iron, ammonia). It prepares water for biological filtration without chemicals — sustainable and fits the mineral-retention philosophy.

INFRASTRUCTURE COMPONENT 04

Sand / Bio-Sand Filter

Capex: ₹3,000 – ₹10,000
Conceptual Schematic Village Implementation

Methodology & Operation

Water percolates through layers of sand/gravel. Physical straining removes particles; in bio-sand version, a biological "schmutzdecke" layer (biofilm) on top biologically degrades organics, ammonia, and pathogens.

Pros

  • Natural Pathogen Red.
  • Dual filtration
  • Low media cost

Cons

  • Slow flow rate
  • 2-4 wk maturation

  • Total Odor Removal
  • Aesthetic polishing
  • Simple install

Cons

  • Adsorption limit
  • Periodic replacement

INFRASTRUCTURE COMPONENT 06

Ultrafiltration (UF)

Capex: ₹10,000 – ₹30,000
Conceptual Schematic Village Implementation

Methodology & Operation

Pressure-driven membrane (pore size ~0.01–0.1 micron) that physically sieves out bacteria, viruses, colloids, and fine particles while allowing dissolved minerals to pass. Gold standard for bio-safety without mineral removal.

Pros

  • 100% Bio-barrier
  • Mineral retention
  • Low energy usage

Cons

  • Fouling risk
  • Backwash needed

BacteriaNil~100% removal~100%

Evaluator's Rationale

Provides robust physical disinfection without removing minerals or creating wastage — perfect balance for this system. Achieves crystal-clear water and ensures complete bacterial safety.

Pros

  • Residual Protection
  • Proven Reliability
  • Extremely Low Cost

Cons

  • Potential Taste
  • Chemical Handling

Overall Evaluator's Verdict

The proposed infrastructure represents a well-balanced, sustainable conventional treatment train. It achieves high-accuracy physical and biological purification while avoiding the specific externalities of RO (high energy, mineral stripping, and high wastewater brine).

Primary Strengths
  • Mineral-retaining design
  • Zero-wastage recovery
  • Low maintenance CAPEX
Optimization Areas
  • Scheduled GAC renewal
  • UF Fouling Monitoring
  • Periodic Desilting

Contextual Fit

Ideal for pond water treatment in the Tamil Nadu region where decentralised, mineral-rich, and low-energy solutions are prioritized over complex industrial osmosis systems.

Reverse Osmosis with
Blending System

An advanced purification approach incorporating high-pressure membrane technology and controlled bypass blending to achieve superior TDS reduction while maintaining essential mineral balance.

STRATEGIC OVERVIEW

System Conceptual Mind Map

RO INFRASTRUCTURE 01

Sedimentation Tank (1000 L)

Capex: ₹5,000 – ₹15,000
Conceptual SchematicVillage Implementation

Methodology

Gravity settling tank where heavy suspended particles, silt, and some organics settle as sludge. Same pre-treatment as conventional system (50–70% turbidity reduction).

Pros

  • Passive / No Energy
  • Protects RO Memb.
  • Low Operating Cost

Cons

  • Slow Process
  • Sludge Mgmt

Evaluator's Rationale

Essential low-cost first step for high-turbidity pond water. Protects expensive RO membranes from fouling and extends their life.

Air bubbled through diffusers to oxidise iron, strip odours, and reduce ammonia. Prepared water for downstream biological and RO stages.

  • Natural Oxidation
  • Improves RO Perf.
  • Removes Odors

Cons

  • Requires Blower
  • Noise Pollution

Evaluator's Rationale

Critical for pond water (iron, low DO, ammonia). Prevents RO membrane fouling and improves overall efficiency.

Bio-Sand Filter

Layered sand/gravel with biological layer for physical straining + biological degradation of organics and pathogens.

  • Robust Turbidity Red.
  • Local Materials
  • Low Media Cost

Cons

  • Slow Flow Rate
  • Maturation Time

Evaluator's Rationale

RO INFRASTRUCTURE 04

Activated Carbon (GAC)

Capex: ₹4,000 – ₹12,000
Conceptual SchematicVillage Implementation

Adsorbs organic compounds, chlorine, and taste/odour molecules via high-surface area granular carbon.

  • Protects RO Memb.

RO INFRASTRUCTURE 05

Ultrafiltration (UF)

Capex: ₹10,000 – ₹30,000
Conceptual SchematicVillage Implementation

Methodology & Split Flow

0.01–0.1 micron physical barrier that removes 100% of bacteria and viruses. Crucially, after this stage, the flow is split: one part proceeds to the RO Membrane (RO Path) while the other bypasses it (Bypass Path) for later blending.

Pros

  • Final Bio-Barrier
  • Enables Bypass Flow
  • High Recovery

Cons

  • Fouling Risk
  • Backwash needed

Evaluator's Rationale

Ensures that both the water being treated by RO and the bypass water are microbiologically sterile before they split.

RO INFRASTRUCTURE 06

Path A: RO Membrane

Capex: ₹25,000 – ₹60,000
Conceptual SchematicVillage Implementation

Methodology: RO Path Performance

This path processes a portion of the UF water through a high-pressure semi-permeable membrane. It removes 90–95% of dissolved salts, heavy metals, and persistent contaminants like Fluoride and Nitrate.

Pros

  • Superior Purity
  • Removes Nitrates
  • Eliminates Salinity

Cons

  • Energy Intensive
  • Brine Waste Flow

Evaluator's Rationale

The RO Path is the key to tackling dissolved solids that filters cannot catch. It produces ultra-clean water for final blending.

RO INFRASTRUCTURE 06B

Path B: Bypass (Filtered)

Capex: ₹1,000 – ₹3,000
Conceptual SchematicVillage Implementation

Methodology: Bypass Stream

A portion of the Ultrafiltered (UF) water is diverted directly to the blending unit. Since it hasn't passed through the RO membrane, it retains all the natural minerals from the original pond water, while remaining microbiologically safe.

Pros

  • Mineral Preservation
  • Zero Energy Cost
  • Zero Water Wastage

Cons

  • High TDS (Untreated salts)
  • Only Path 1 partial

Evaluator's Rationale

The Bypass Path ensures that the final water isn't "sterile and dead." It provides the essential salts that give water its neutral, healthy profile.

RO INFRASTRUCTURE 07

Mixing & Blending Unit

Capex: ₹6,000 – ₹15,000
Conceptual SchematicVillage Implementation

Methodology: Re-Convergence

This stage merges the ultra-low TDS RO Water with the mineral-rich Bypass Filtered Water. By adjusting the mixing ratio, the system achieves a target TDS of 300–450 mg/L.

Pros

  • Restores Minerals
  • Perfect Taste Profile
  • Operational Flex.

Cons

  • Flow Calibration
  • Ratio Management

Evaluator's Rationale

Blending is the secret to "Balanced Mineral Water." It ensures high-purity without stripping essential salts like calcium and magnesium.

Nanofiltration Series · Version 2026.04

Nanofiltration (NF)
System Infrastructure

The state-of-the-art middle-ground: Optimized for superior hardness removal and nitrate reduction with significantly better mineral retention than RO.

STRATEGIC OVERVIEW

System Conceptual Mind Map

FIG 3.0: NF INTEGRATED FLOW & MINERAL BALANCE LOGIC
NF INFRASTRUCTURE 01

Screening

Capex: ₹500 – ₹2,000
Conceptual SchematicVillage Implementation

Methodology: Coarse Straining

Coarse mesh or bar screen at the inlet removes large debris (leaves, twigs, plastics) from raw pond water before it enters the sedimentation tank. It is an essential passive shield for downstream units.

Pros

  • Simple and passive
  • Prevents clogging
  • Zero operating cost

Cons

  • Visible debris only
  • Requires manual cleaning

Why Selected

Essential first protective step for high-debris pond water. Keeps the entire system (especially the NF membrane) running smoothly at almost zero cost.

NF INFRASTRUCTURE 02

Sedimentation Tank (1000 L)

Capex: ₹5,000 – ₹15,000
Conceptual SchematicVillage Implementation

Methodology: Gravity-Based Settling

Gravity-based settling tank allowing heavy suspended particles and silt to settle as sludge (Turbidity ↓50–70%). Completely passive pre-treatment.

Pros

  • No energy or chemicals
  • Reduces filter load
  • Simple construction

Cons

  • Slow process time
  • Regular desilting req.

Why Selected

Low-cost, effective pre-treatment for turbid pond water. Protects the expensive NF membrane from fouling and improves overall plant uptime.

NF INFRASTRUCTURE 03

Diffused Aeration Unit

Capex: ₹8,000 – ₹25,000
Conceptual SchematicVillage Implementation

Methodology: Technical Oxidation

Air is diffused into water to increase dissolved oxygen, oxidise iron, and reduce persistent ammonia and odour. Oxidation turns soluble iron into filterable solids.

Pros

  • Natural oxidation
  • Removes odours
  • Improves taste

Cons

  • Requires blower power
  • Minor equipment noise

Why Selected

Pond water often contains high iron, ammonia, and odour compounds. Aeration is critical to prepare the water for high-performance membrane treatment.

NF INFRASTRUCTURE 04

Sand / Bio-Sand Filter

Capex: ₹3,000 – ₹10,000
Conceptual SchematicVillage Implementation

Methodology: Bio-Filter Barrier

Multi-layer sand filter with a biological layer on top for physical straining + natural biodegradation. Achieves 80–95% turbidity and 60–90% bacteria reduction.

Pros

  • Nature-based solution
  • Low media cost
  • Pathogen reduction

Cons

  • Slower flow rate
  • Cleaning downtime

Why Selected

A strong pre-treatment step that significantly reduces organic load, protecting the NF and UF membranes from bio-fouling.

NF INFRASTRUCTURE 05

Activated Carbon Filter (GAC)

Capex: ₹4,000 – ₹12,000
Conceptual SchematicVillage Implementation

Methodology: Adsorption Polishing

Granular activated carbon adsorbs organic compounds, chlorine, colour, taste and odour. Dramatically improves the aesthetic quality of pond-derived water.

Pros

  • Crystal clear clarity
  • Total odour removal
  • Simple operation

Cons

  • Media saturates
  • Periodic replacement

Why Selected

Removes organics and chlorine-demanding substances before the membrane stages, significantly extending NF filter life.

NF INFRASTRUCTURE 06

Ultrafiltration (UF)

Capex: ₹10,000 – ₹30,000
Conceptual SchematicVillage Implementation

Methodology: Secondary Barrier

Fine membrane (0.01–0.1 micron) removes 100% of bacteria and viruses while allowing healthy dissolved minerals to pass. Acts as the final pre-filter for NF.

Pros

  • Complete bio-shield
  • Low energy usage
  • Long life pre-filter

Cons

  • Fouling sensitive
  • Backwash needed

Why Selected

Delivers high-purity feed water to the NF membrane, ensuring its longevity and maintaining 100% microbiological safety.

NF INFRASTRUCTURE 07

Nanofiltration (NF) Membrane

Capex: ₹35,000 – ₹80,000
Conceptual SchematicVillage Implementation

Methodology: Partial Desalination

A pressure-driven membrane that selectively removes hardness, monovalent salts, and nitrates while allowing bone-benefitting minerals to pass through.

Pros

  • Low Energy vs RO
  • Less water wastage
  • Balanced Minerals

Cons

  • Produces reject flow
  • Partial TDS reduction

Why Selected

The core innovation of this system — it provides effective desalination and hardness removal with a much smaller environmental footprint than full RO.

NF INFRASTRUCTURE 08

Chlorination Unit

Capex: ₹2,000 – ₹8,000
Conceptual SchematicVillage Implementation

Methodology: Disinfection

Final chemical dosing to kill residual microbes and provide an ongoing barrier against re-contamination during distribution.

Pros

  • Extremely low cost
  • Reliable protection
  • Residual safety

Cons

  • Chemical dosing care
  • Mild residual taste

Why Selected

Ensures 100% microbiological safety from the system output to the final community distribution faucet.

Storage Tank (2000 L)

Conceptual SchematicVillage Implementation

  • Cleaning schedule

Required for practical village water security and efficient management of production cycles.
